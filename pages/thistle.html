Research

My research interests span various fields of the extragalactic Universe. Most recently, my focus is on the formation and evolution of massive galaxies at high redshift. Specifically, my projects include:

Legacy field surveys: Deep imaging and spectroscopic programmes provide our best understandings of the high-redshift Universe. I work closely with multi-wavelength data, including JWST, VISTA, HST, ALMA, and NOEMA. I am also leading the THISTLE survey, one of the highest time allocations among VLA Large programmes for high-redshift physics.

Cosmic evolution of dust-obscured star formation: I am interested in how dusty galaxies contribute to the star-forming budget at a cosmological level. We provide one of the tightest observational constraints on the dust-obscured cosmic SFRD.

Gravitational lensing: I love lensing.

The THISTLE survey

The THISTLE (Triple-tuning High-redshift Interferometric STrong LEnsing) survey is a VLA Large Programme approved in the 2026B semester. It targets the lensing cluster field Abell 370.

The project is expected to span three years. In 2026B, it is allocated approximately 160 hours with Priority A status. THISTLE has two main science goals:

  • Molecular gas from cosmic dawn to cosmic noon, traced through CO(1–0) and CO(2–1).
  • Radio continuum constraints across redshift for more than 16,000 galaxies within the coverage.
