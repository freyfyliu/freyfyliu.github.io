Feng-Yuan Frey Liu   刘丰源

PhD candidate at the University of Edinburgh

Research

My research interests span various fields of the extragalactic Universe. Most recently, my focus is on the formation and evolution of massive galaxies at high redshift. Specifically, my projects include:

Legacy field surveys: Deep imaging and spectroscopic programmes provide our best understandings of the high-redshift Universe. I work closely with multi-wavelength data, including JWST, VISTA, HST, ALMA, and NOEMA. I am also leading the THISTLE survey, one of the highest time allocations among VLA Large programmes for high-redshift physics.

Cosmic evolution of dust-obscured star formation: I am interested in how dusty galaxies contribute to the star-forming budget at a cosmological level. We provide one of the tightest observational constraints on the dust-obscured cosmic SFRD.

Gravitational lensing: I love lensing.

Career path

Currently, I am conducting my PhD study at the Royal Observatory Edinburgh, at the heart of Scotland. I finished my undergraduate study at Nanjing University, and then my Master's at the National Astronomical Observatories, Chinese Academy of Sciences. You can find more details in my CV.

My work has taken me to over twenty countries in four continents, and I am still exploring more. You can find some imagery proof in my gallery.

Teaching

Teaching and educating is one of my biggest passions, including:

Supervising: I enjoy working with students, mostly through undergraduate summer projects.

Lecturing: I enjoy translating observational techniques and galaxy-evolution physics into accessible training material.

Summer school organisation: I organised ESEA-I, the first Edinburgh School of Extragalactic Astronomy.
