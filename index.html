Research

My research interests span various fields of the extragalactic Universe. Most recently, my focus is on the formation and evolution of massive galaxies at high redshift. Specifically, my projects include:

  • Legacy field surveys Deep imaging/spectroscopic programmes provide our best understandings of the high-redshift Universe, and I work closely with various data at multi-wavelengths, including JWST (e.g. as a member of the PRIMER survey), VISTA (e.g. UltraVISTA), HST (e.g. CANDELS), ALMA (e.g. A3COSMOS), NOEMA. I am also leading the THISTLE survey, one of the highest time allocations among VLA Large programmes for high-redshift physics.
  • Cosmic evolution of dust-obscured star formation I am interested in how the dusty galaxies contribute to the star-forming budget at a cosmological level. We provide one of the tightest observational constraints of the dust-obscured cosmic SFRD.
  • Gravitational lensing I love lensing.
